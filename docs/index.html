What Is Tai Chi Chuan?

Tai Chi Chuan, also known as Taiji Quan, translates to 'Grand Ultimate Fist.' This internal Chinese marital art is recognized as a distinctive internal style of Kung Fu.

The practice involves slow, graceful movements performed in a relaxed yet controlled manner. Common Tai Chi practices encompass spiraling silk reeling exercises, standing and sitting qigong meditation, and two-person push hands (tui shou) practice.

This video showcases the Chen-style 48 movement form performed at a brisk speed.
